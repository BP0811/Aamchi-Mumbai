Description

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument completed in 1924 on the waterfront of Mumbai (Bombay), India. It was erected to commemorate the landing of George V for his coronation as the Emperor of India in December 1911 at Strand Road near Wellington Fountain. He was the first British monarch to visit India. Located on the waterfront at Apollo Bunder area at the end of Chhatrapati Shivaji Marg in South Mumbai and overlooks the Arabian Sea, This monument has also been referred to as the Taj Mahal of Mumbai, and is the city's top tourist attraction. Gateway of India is one of the most important landmarks in Mumbai and primarily a top attraction on every tourist’s list thanks to its grandeur and undying charm. The structure serves as a beautiful remembrance of the rich cultural heritage and it's colonial past. Gateway of India is the first structure to welcome visitors entering by sea and is popularly known as the Taj Mahal of Mumbai. Apart from its’ historic importance, it is also known for its riveting sight of stunning yachts and boats sailing in the azure sea. Gateway of India gleams like an uncrowned prince especially on rainy days as well as during sunrises and sunsets.The Gateway of India holds a prominent place in India's history and is an iconic monument located in Mumbai (formerly known as Bombay), Maharashtra. It stands as a testament to the city's rich architectural heritage and colonial past. The history of the Gateway of India dates back to the early 20th century when it was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Mumbai in 1911.
