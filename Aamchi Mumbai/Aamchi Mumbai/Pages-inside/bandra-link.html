Description

The Bandra-Worli Sea Link, officially known as the Rajiv Gandhi Sea Link, is an iconic 5.6-kilometer cable-stayed bridge connecting Bandra in Western Mumbai to Worli in South Mumbai, opened in 2009. As an engineering marvel, it features striking pylons and spans offering breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea and the Mumbai skyline, especially captivating at night when illuminated. Symbolizing Mumbai's progress and modernity, the Sea Link significantly reduces travel time between key city areas. Accessible year-round, the best times to visit are early morning or late evening to avoid traffic and enjoy serene views. Nearby attractions include the historic Bandra Fort and the scenic Worli Sea Face. While stopping on the bridge for photos is prohibited for safety, designated viewpoints at both ends provide excellent photo opportunities, making the Sea Link a must-visit landmark in Mumbai.

The Sea Link provides breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea and the expansive Mumbai skyline. These views are particularly enchanting during sunrise and sunset when the bridge and its surroundings are bathed in golden light. At night, the Sea Link is beautifully illuminated, creating a stunning reflection on the water and adding to the city's vibrant nightlife scenery. This illumination makes it a perfect spot for nighttime photography and sightseeing.
